Larry Page & Sergey Brin

Larry Page and Sergey Brin were two Stanford University students who studied computer science. In 1995 they began working on a research project which was to explore the mathematical properties of the World Wide Web, focusing on how webpages are interconnected.
During this research they developed an algorithm called PageRank. PageRank analyzed and compared different websites by looking at their number and quality of links pointing to a webpage, this provided a way to rank webpages based on their importance and relevance. This was the beginning of the concept which Google would be built upon.

Before Google was known as Google, the name they gave it was BackRub. It operated on Stanford servers for a while, and showcased the power of their PageRank algorithm by delivering more relevant search results compared to other search engines at the time.
In 1998 they decided to start a company which they called Google, Google was a play on the word "googol" which refers to the number 1 followed by 100 zeros. Their first workplace was located in a garage in Mento Park, California.

When Google went public in 2004, it was a huge success which raised billions of dollars making Larry and Sergey billionaires and with this financial backing they expanded their services acquiring companies like YouTube and Android. They also launched products such as Google Maps and the popular web browser Google Chrome.
Further on Google continued to innovate, developing products and services like artificial intelligence, cloud computing (Google Cloud) and self-driving cars. (Waymo).

Larry Page and Sergey Brin's vision, technical expertise and commitment were crucial in the creation and success of Google, making it an integral part of Internet's Evolution.
